RoundUp

UI/UX & Interaction Design, 2018

RoundUp is a campus engagement tool for colleges and universities. The iOS application is positioned as a campus-wide game, as the objective is to discover university events, check in via geolocation, and collect points to win prizes. My mission was to design a fun, engaging experience that would promote campus involvement, and entice students to leave their dorm rooms! I led design on this project from concept to launch, and explored my creative bounds through research and HCD.

I started by stripping the idea down to its core â€“ event discovery & point collection. The event feed would consist of a few trending events, and a carousel of upcoming events, sorted by date. The app's format was inspired Facebook Paper, a defunct Facebook project. I liked the idea of displaying minimal information on first sight, and more detail upon further exploration.

The idea of gamifying event attendance led my visualization of large bold fonts, strong colors, and bouncy animations from the jump. This was a unique project for me because it allowed me to focus more on playful design, as opposed to sleek, corporate interfaces. I used Emojis as category indicators, and a cool Snapchat hack to display Bitmojis as user avatars, which are displayed on the point leaderboard and throughout the app.

RoundUp has become a tool that many universities throughout the United States are using to increase student involvement, and gamify event engagement. It's been amazing to see students on these campuses quickly adopting RoundUp as their medium for event invitations and discovery!
